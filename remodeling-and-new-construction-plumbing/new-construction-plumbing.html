Frequently Asked Questions

The most commonly used pipes in new constructions are PEX (cross-linked polyethylene), PVC (polyvinyl chloride), and copper pipes, each offering durability and reliability.

To ensure energy efficiency, use high-efficiency fixtures, insulate hot water pipes, and consider a tankless water heater to reduce energy consumption and water waste.

Consider the layout and size of your home, local building codes, water pressure requirements, and future maintenance accessibility when designing a new plumbing system.

Prevent leaks by using high-quality materials, ensuring proper installation, conducting pressure testing, and performing regular inspections after the construction is complete.

A professional plumber ensures the plumbing system is designed and installed correctly according to code, helping to prevent costly repairs and ensuring long-term efficiency and safety.
