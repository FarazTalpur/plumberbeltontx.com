Frequently Asked Questions

It is recommended to test your sump pump at least once every three months and before the rainy season. You can do this by pouring a bucket of water into the sump pit to ensure it starts automatically and properly discharges the water.

Regular maintenance includes cleaning the sump pit, checking the pump for debris, ensuring the float switch operates smoothly, inspecting the discharge line for clogs, and checking the power source to ensure the pump is receiving power.

Signs of malfunction include unusual noises, vibrations, infrequent cycling, the pump running but not ejecting water, or a musty smell indicating standing water. Regular testing can help catch these issues early.

Yes, having a backup system is highly recommended. A battery backup or a water-powered backup pump can help ensure your basement remains dry during a power outage or primary pump failure.

Sump pumps typically last about 7-10 years. Consider replacement if your pump is near this age or exhibiting persistent issues despite maintenance, such as frequent cycling, unusual noises, or failure to run.
